Massive Starlet Coral (Big Red)

On site in Bocas del Toro, Panama, Karl Castillo's research lab collects coral cores, samples of corals shaped like cylinders that the researchers transport back to their lab at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. Each core is a timeline dating back years in a coral's life and health history. Layer by layer, the researchers measure the density of each coral's skeleton and how that changed over time, allowing them to determine whether the coral's environment was suitable for growth that year.

Dissecting the Coral

Instructions: An isotope proxy is a sample of a coral layer marine ecologists use to determine temperature and other environmental factors that affect coral growth rates. Based on industry standard equations, researchers can determine the temperature, precipitation level, pollution level, and freshness or saltiness of the water around that coral for each rainy and dry season of each year of a coral's life.
