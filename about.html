Students in UNC-Chapel Hill’s School of Media and Journalism are honored to tell the stories of change in Bocas del Toro, Panama.

The generous support of the university’s department of Marine Sciences aided the project and its goal to tell the story of sea warming in the Caribbean and the effect it has on coral ecosystems.

What the team uncovered were the undercurrents of change in Bocas del Toro: growing signs of environmental harm, economic pressure and social division in the region. Some of the changes are subtle and slow, like the ebbing of the social structures that divide Panamanians. Other engulf the province. But this province is resilient and the people, the creatures, the land are swimming with and against the currents of change.
